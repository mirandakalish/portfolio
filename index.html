2D Work

Color Wheel
Fall 2013
Acrylic

Our assignment was to make a color wheel with twelve identical slices using multiple shapes in each slice. Each slice consists of the color, its complement, and various tints, shades, and saturation levels of the color.

"The Athlete"
Fall 2014
Charcoal

Our assignment was to arrange a still life using objects that fit into a certain persona or identity. I included a skateboard, weight, batting glove, water bottle, and varsity jacket.

Figure Drawing
Spring 2014
Charcoal

The goal of this figure session was to explore the contrast created through the light falling on the model's body.

"Note to Self"
Fall 2013
Post-it Notes on Cork

Our assignment was to explore any project idea we wanted, but it had to explore the interaction of color. I collected post-it notes from my friends and myself, most of which were not created for the sole purpose of this project, but had been used to take notes.

3 Point Perspective
Spring 2014
Charcoal

My drawing class took a trip to downtown St. Louis with the intended task of finding compositions to use for a 3-point perspective drawing. This drawing is of a parking lot structure in Busch Stadium.

Texture
Fall 2013
Micron Pen

Our assignment was to document textures we encountered throughout our daily lives. Then, we were to choose one and use a repeated mark in 7 values from black to white to create our texture with pen. My repeated mark was a single dot.

Interpretive Self-Portrait
Spring 2014
Soft Pastels

Our assignment was to do a portrait that was more than just a depiction of someone, but rather should tell viewers something about that person. I decided to draw myself having trouble deciding what cereal to buy, as making decisions is a struggle I often deal with.

Illustrated Letter
Fall 2014
Etching Ink

We began this project by writing out sans serif and serif alphabets with a calligraphy pen at different angles. We chose 5 calligraphy letters we found aesthetically interesting, and explored the weight, contrast, and width of them. Eventually, we settled on a base form for one of the letters and added illustrative elements to it. We then used pronto plates to make prints of the letters.

Digital Work

Me, Myself, and I
Fall 2012

This was a piece I did for my college entrance portfolio. I am very interested in how type can act as and take the shape of image, and I explored that in this piece.

Underwater Heraldry
Fall 2014

Our assignment was to make a pattern in photoshop using one word from each of 2 lists. I chose the words "underwater" and "heraldry" and made a drawing that I then scanned, colored, and offset. Then I drew a center object and scanned and colored it as well.

Icons (Shark, Pterodactyl, Pizza)
Fall 2014

Our assignment was to pick three objects from a list and make 3 atered icons that expressed the objects. The top icon had to be a silhouette, the middle icon could use white, black, and red, and the bottom icon could be no larger than 1x1 inches.

Life in Color
Summer 2014

For this project, I decided to take images of places and everyday objects and reduce them to 4 of the most prominent colors that people may associate with them. My goal is to do more of these and see if I can truly evoke the essence of the place/object.

Typography
Fall 2012

This was another piece I did for my college entrance portfolio. My main design interest lies with type, and this was an exercise in the interaction of typefaces and position.

Issues Magazine Layout
Fall 2014

This is a spread I laid out for Issues Magazine, an Urban Design publication at Washington University.

Typography
Fall 2012

This was my favorite piece from my college entrance portfolio. I am a huge baseball fan, and I decided to do some illustration related to the sport. I started by hand and then scanned and edited the drawing in photoshop. I plan to re-do it now that I also know how to use illustrator.

Collage Self-Portrait
Fall 2014

Our assignment was to do a self-portrait through collaging various photographs, drawings, appropriated images, etc. The portrait was supposed to say something about our interests and personalities.
